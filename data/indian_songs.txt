INDIAN AND BOLLYWOOD CHORD PROGRESSIONS AND SONGS

Much of Bollywood, Indian pop, and Indie music uses the same Western chord progressions as global pop, so the emotional logic is similar. Guitarists playing Indian songs use standard major and minor chords and common progressions.

The I-V-vi-IV progression and the vi-IV-I-V progression are extremely common in modern Bollywood and Indian pop ballads, giving the same emotional, uplifting, or bittersweet feel as in Western music.

Songs with a warm, romantic, or nostalgic feel: "Tum Hi Ho" (Arijit Singh), "Kal Ho Naa Ho" (Sonu Nigam), "Pee Loon" (Mohit Chauhan), "Tujhe Kitna Chahne Lage" (Arijit Singh).

Songs with a melancholic or longing feel: "Channa Mereya" (Arijit Singh), "Agar Tum Saath Ho" (Arijit Singh, Alka Yagnik), "Phir Le Aya Dil" (Arijit Singh), "Kabira" (Tochi Raina, Rekha Bhardwaj).

Songs with an upbeat, hopeful, or celebratory feel: "Ilahi" (Arijit Singh), "Gallan Goodiyaan" (from Dil Dhadakne Do), "Nashe Si Chadh Gayi" (Arijit Singh), "Matargashti" (Mohit Chauhan).

Indie and acoustic-friendly Indian songs commonly played on guitar: "Iktara" (Amit Trivedi), "Phir Se Ud Chala" (Mohit Chauhan), "Khairiyat" (Arijit Singh), "Choo Lo" (The Local Train), "Kho Gaye Hum Kahan" (Prateek Kuhad).

Prateek Kuhad and The Local Train are popular Indian indie artists whose songs use simple, guitar-friendly open-chord progressions and are good references for an acoustic, heartfelt feel.
